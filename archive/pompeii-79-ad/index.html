Instance 02 — Campania, 79 AD

The Last Day of Pompeii

You had eighteen hours to leave. Everyone who stayed had a reason that sounded like sense.

✦

“The mountain gave roughly eighteen hours of notice before it killed anyone in Pompeii. I have filed the twelve reasons people spent those hours packing, sheltering, praying, and waiting for boats. Sources attached — hindsight deserves paperwork.”— Orphea, Archive Administrator

On a day in 79 AD, Mount Vesuvius erupted for roughly twenty-five hours over the Bay of Naples. Most of Pompeii's ten-to-twenty thousand residents escaped; the roughly 1,150 bodies recovered so far belonged, overwhelmingly, to people who left too late or could not leave at all. The first pyroclastic surge did not reach Pompeii itself until the following dawn. Twelve of the mechanisms that decided who walked out and who became a plaster cast are filed below, with sources.
