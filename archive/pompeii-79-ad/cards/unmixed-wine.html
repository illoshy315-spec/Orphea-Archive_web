Record 12 · Instance 02 — Campania, 79 AD

The drink that felt like rescue

Romans watered their wine four to one. Drinking it straight was what barbarians did — everyone knew that.

Pompeii was a wine city: vineyards up the mountain’s own slopes, bars on every block. And Roman custom kept that ocean of alcohol behind a cultural safety rail — wine was drunk diluted, four to six parts water to one of wine. Straight wine, merum, was for drunkards, gladiators, and Gauls.

The rail exists because of what straight alcohol does: it feels like strength and warmth arriving at the exact moment it is taking your judgment away. In a disaster, that trade is lethal.
