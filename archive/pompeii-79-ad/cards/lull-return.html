Record 05 · Instance 02 — Campania, 79 AD

The mountain paused for breath. They heard a full stop.

The stone stopped falling. For a little while — perhaps half an hour — the city was quiet.

Survivors of the first night climbed out of second-floor windows onto streets buried in loose rock, and moved — some toward the gates, some back toward home to see what was left.

The bodies found lying on top of the pumice layer belong to them: people who survived eighteen hours of falling stone, and then trusted the silence.
