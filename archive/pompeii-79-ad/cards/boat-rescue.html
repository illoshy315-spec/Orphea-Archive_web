Record 02 · Instance 02 — Campania, 79 AD

Three hundred people waited for boats that never landed

The rescue fleet was real. It was on the water. The sea said no.

In Herculaneum, hundreds gathered on the beach and packed into twelve stone boat sheds facing the water — the logical assembly point for an evacuation by sea. The Roman navy really was coming: the admiral at Misenum had launched warships across the bay.

But wind and waves made the shore unreachable. Around one in the morning, the first pyroclastic surge came down the mountain at several hundred degrees. Everyone in the sheds died where they waited.
