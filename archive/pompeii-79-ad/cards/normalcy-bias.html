Record 04 · Instance 02 — Campania, 79 AD

Seventeen years of earthquakes taught them not to run

The ground had shaken for days. Nobody packed. It had shaken before, and nothing had come of it.

Seventeen years earlier, a massive earthquake had wrecked Pompeii — and the city survived, rebuilt, carried on. Scaffolding from those repairs was still up in 79 AD. Tremors were, in Pliny’s words, “not particularly alarming, because they are frequent in Campania.”

So when the ground rumbled for days before the eruption, experience gave its confident, fatal advice: this passes. It always has.
