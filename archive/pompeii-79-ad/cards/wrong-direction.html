Record 07 · Instance 02 — Campania, 79 AD

South was the road the ash took

Running from the mountain and running from the eruption were two different directions.

High-altitude wind pushed the eruption column southeast — directly over Pompeii and the coast beyond it. Fleeing south meant traveling with the fallout, under it, for hours.

The record keeps the score: survivors’ names resurface in towns to the north, while the bodies of the late escapees have been found along the roads south.
