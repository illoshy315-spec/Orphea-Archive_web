Record 08 · Instance 02 — Campania, 79 AD

Armor for the wrong enemy

They tied pillows to their heads and walked out under the falling stone. It worked.

Pliny’s letters preserve the image: households leaving Stabiae with cushions bound to their heads with cloth, helmets against the pumice. Against falling stone, it was genuinely sound protection.

But the eruption’s real killer wasn’t falling. The pyroclastic surges arrived as a wave of gas and ash at several hundred degrees — and no protection anyone could improvise, or even imagine, addressed heat.
