Record 01 · Instance 02 — Campania, 79 AD

Staying indoors was the deadliest plan in town

Four walls, a solid roof — and fifteen centimeters of stone landing on that roof every hour.

When pumice started falling on Pompeii, the obvious move was the one that works in a thunderstorm: get inside, wait it out. But the fall didn’t stop. It accumulated — nearly three meters of it by morning.

Roofs caved in under the load. Doors jammed shut behind drifts of stone. The house became a sealed container, filling from above.
