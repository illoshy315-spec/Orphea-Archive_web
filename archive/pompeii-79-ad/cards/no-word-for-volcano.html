Record 09 · Instance 02 — Campania, 79 AD

Nobody knew the mountain was a volcano

Romans knew what volcanoes were. Etna smoked on every sailor’s horizon. Vesuvius just wasn’t on the list.

Vesuvius had been quiet for centuries — long past living memory. Its slopes were vineyards; towns crowded its base. When Pliny tried to describe the eruption cloud, he reached for a garden image: a Mediterranean pine.

Every warning sign — the tremors, the failing springs — was legible only in a category no one had applied. You cannot evacuate from a threat your language hasn’t attached to your mountain.
