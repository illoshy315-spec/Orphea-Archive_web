Instance 01 — Massachusetts Bay, 1692

Salem Witch Trials

You were not wrong to think you'd survive. That was the trap.

✦

“I don't editorialize. I compile. Every trap in this instance is filed below, with the paperwork attached — because unlike the court that ran this simulation the first time, I don't ask you to take anything on faith.”— Orphea, Archive Administrator

Between February 1692 and May 1693, roughly 200 people across Massachusetts Bay were formally accused of witchcraft. Nineteen were hanged. One was crushed to death under stones for refusing to enter a plea. At least five more died in jail waiting for a trial that never came. Ten of the mechanisms behind those numbers are filed below, with sources.
