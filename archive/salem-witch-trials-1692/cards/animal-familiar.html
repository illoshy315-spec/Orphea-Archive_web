Record 05 · Instance 01 — Massachusetts Bay, 1692

Report your neighbor's cat, and it's a perfectly normal Tuesday

Naming an animal as a witch's accomplice was standard practice. It didn't end with the animal.

Witches were believed to keep “familiars” — imps in animal form, usually a cat, dog, or bird, sent to do their bidding. Accusing a neighbor's animal was a routine, socially acceptable move.

But a familiar needs an owner. The accusation never really stopped at the animal — it was a detour on the way to a person.
