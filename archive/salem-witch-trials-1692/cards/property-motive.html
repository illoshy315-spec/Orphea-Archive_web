Record 08 · Instance 01 — Massachusetts Bay, 1692

The more land you owned, the more likely you were a witch

A guilty verdict could turn your farm into the county’s farm.

A conviction could result in the Crown seizing the condemned’s property. Landowners — especially women who’d inherited land — were disproportionately represented among the accused.

Producing a deed to prove your standing could backfire: it told the court exactly how much there was to gain from your conviction.
