Record 06 · Instance 01 — Massachusetts Bay, 1692

Vouching for a neighbor could put your own name in the file

Rebecca Nurse had 39 signatures testifying to her character. She was hanged anyway.

Community petitions attesting to an accused person's good character were a real, documented defense strategy — and a reasonable one.

They didn't reliably work, and signing one carried its own quiet risk: associating your name with someone already under suspicion.
